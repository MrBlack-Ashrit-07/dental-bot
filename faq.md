Q: What are your clinic's operating hours?
A: We're open Monday through Saturday from 9:00 AM to 8:00 PM, with a midday break between 1:00 PM and 2:00 PM. We're closed on Sundays and major public holidays.

Q: Do I need to walk in, or should I always book an appointment?
A: We strongly recommend booking an appointment in advance to minimize waiting time. We do accommodate walk-ins when slots are available, but appointment holders are always given priority.

Q: Is the clinic open on public holidays?
A: We remain closed on major national holidays such as Republic Day, Independence Day, Gandhi Jayanti, Diwali, and Holi. Any holiday closures are posted in advance on our website and WhatsApp updates.

Q: How do I book an appointment?
A: You can book through our website, by calling the front desk, or via WhatsApp. We'll confirm your slot and send a reminder a day before your visit.

Q: How far in advance should I book?
A: For routine check-ups, booking 2–3 days ahead is usually sufficient. For specialized procedures like implants or orthodontic consultations, we recommend booking at least a week in advance.

Q: What should I bring to my first appointment?
A: Please bring a valid photo ID, any previous dental records or X-rays, a list of current medications, and your insurance card if applicable.

Q: How long does a typical appointment last?
A: A routine check-up and cleaning usually takes 30–45 minutes. Procedures like fillings or root canals can take 45 minutes to over an hour, depending on complexity.

Q: Can I request a specific dentist?
A: Yes, you're welcome to request a specific dentist when booking. We'll do our best to match your preference with available slots.

Q: Do you send appointment reminders?
A: Yes, we send SMS and WhatsApp reminders 24 hours before your scheduled visit, along with a follow-up reminder on the morning of your appointment.

Q: What is your cancellation policy?
A: We request at least 24 hours' notice for cancellations or rescheduling. This allows us to offer the slot to another patient who may need urgent care.

Q: Is there a fee for late cancellations or no-shows?
A: A nominal fee of ₹300–₹500 may apply for repeated no-shows or cancellations made with less than 4 hours' notice. First-time occurrences are generally waived.

Q: Can I reschedule my appointment online?
A: Yes, you can reschedule through our website booking portal or by messaging us on WhatsApp, without needing to call.

Q: Do you offer emergency dental services?
A: Yes, we reserve same-day emergency slots for severe pain, swelling, trauma, or broken teeth. Please call ahead so we can prepare for your arrival.

Q: What counts as a dental emergency?
A: Situations like a knocked-out tooth, uncontrolled bleeding, severe swelling, a broken denture causing pain, or an abscess are considered emergencies and should be addressed immediately.

Q: Is emergency care available after regular hours?
A: We maintain an emergency contact number for after-hours situations. While we can't perform full procedures outside clinic hours, we can guide you on immediate steps and arrange the earliest possible slot.

Q: What should I do if I knock out a tooth?
A: Rinse the tooth gently without scrubbing it, try to place it back in the socket if possible, or keep it in milk or saline, and reach the clinic within 30 minutes for the best chance of saving it.

Q: Do you accept health insurance?
A: Yes, we work with most major insurers and offer cashless treatment through several TPAs (Third Party Administrators). Please share your policy details ahead of your visit so we can verify coverage.

Q: How do I know if my treatment is covered by insurance?
A: Our front desk team can check your policy's coverage for specific procedures before treatment begins, so there are no surprises regarding out-of-pocket costs.

Q: What payment methods do you accept?
A: We accept cash, debit/credit cards, UPI (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm), and net banking. EMI options are also available for higher-cost treatments like implants and orthodontics.

Q: Do you offer EMI or installment plans for expensive procedures?
A: Yes, for treatments above ₹15,000, we offer EMI options through partnered finance providers, typically over 3, 6, or 12 months.

Q: Will I get a detailed bill or invoice for insurance reimbursement?
A: Yes, we provide an itemized invoice with treatment codes and clinic registration details, which you can submit to your insurer for reimbursement.

Q: How much does a routine dental check-up cost?
A: A standard check-up and consultation typically costs between ₹300 and ₹600, depending on whether X-rays are required.

Q: What is the price range for common procedures?
A: Fillings range from ₹800–₹2,500, root canals from ₹3,000–₹8,000, extractions from ₹500–₹3,000, and crowns from ₹3,000–₹15,000 depending on material. We'll always provide a written estimate before starting treatment.

Q: Are cost estimates provided before treatment begins?
A: Absolutely. We discuss the full treatment plan and provide a written cost estimate before any procedure, so you can make an informed decision.

Q: At what age should my child have their first dental visit?
A: We recommend a child's first visit by age one, or within six months of their first tooth erupting, to establish good habits early.

Q: Do you have a dedicated pediatric dentist?
A: Yes, our clinic has a dentist specially trained in pediatric care who uses child-friendly techniques and a calm, reassuring approach.

Q: Do you offer fluoride treatments or sealants for kids?
A: Yes, we offer fluoride applications and dental sealants to protect children's teeth from cavities, typically recommended once permanent molars erupt.

Q: What orthodontic options do you offer?
A: We offer traditional metal braces, ceramic braces, and clear aligners, depending on your bite correction needs, budget, and aesthetic preference.

Q: How long does orthodontic treatment usually take?
A: Treatment duration varies from 12 to 24 months depending on the complexity of misalignment and the type of appliance chosen.

Q: What is the cost of braces or clear aligners?
A: Metal braces typically range from ₹25,000–₹45,000, ceramic braces from ₹40,000–₹70,000, and clear aligners from ₹60,000–₹1,50,000, depending on case complexity.

Q: Can adults get orthodontic treatment too?
A: Yes, there's no upper age limit for orthodontic treatment as long as the gums and bone are healthy enough to support tooth movement.

Q: What cosmetic dental treatments do you offer?
A: We offer teeth whitening, veneers, dental bonding, smile makeovers, and gum contouring to enhance the appearance of your smile.

Q: How long do cosmetic results typically last?
A: Veneers can last 10–15 years with proper care, while bonding may need touch-ups every 3–5 years. Whitening results typically last 6 months to 2 years depending on diet and habits.

Q: Is a consultation required before cosmetic treatment?
A: Yes, we always begin with a consultation to assess your dental health, discuss your goals, and recommend the most suitable treatment plan.

Q: How much does professional teeth whitening cost?
A: In-clinic whitening sessions typically range from ₹4,000–₹10,000, while take-home whitening kits provided by us range from ₹2,500–₹5,000.

Q: Is teeth whitening safe?
A: Yes, when performed under professional supervision, whitening is safe for most patients. We assess for sensitivity, existing restorations, and gum health beforehand.

Q: How long do whitening results last?
A: Results generally last between 6 months and 2 years, depending on your diet, smoking habits, and oral hygiene routine.

Q: What is the success rate of dental implants?
A: Dental implants have a success rate of around 95–98% when placed by experienced professionals and maintained with good oral hygiene.

Q: How long does the implant process take from start to finish?
A: The full process, including healing and crown placement, typically takes 3–6 months, though timelines vary based on bone density and healing.

Q: What is the cost of a single dental implant?
A: A single implant including the crown typically ranges from ₹25,000 to ₹50,000, depending on the implant brand and materials used.

Q: Is the implant procedure painful?
A: The procedure is performed under local anesthesia, so discomfort during placement is minimal. Mild soreness for a few days afterward is normal and managed with prescribed medication.

Q: How do I know if I need a root canal?
A: Signs include persistent tooth pain, sensitivity to hot or cold that lingers, swelling, or darkening of the tooth. An X-ray during consultation confirms the diagnosis.

Q: Is root canal treatment painful?
A: Modern root canal treatment is performed under local anesthesia and is generally no more uncomfortable than getting a filling. Most patients report significant pain relief afterward.

Q: How many visits does a root canal typically require?
A: Most root canals are completed in 1–2 visits, depending on the tooth's condition and whether a crown is needed afterward.

Q: What materials are used for dental crowns?
A: We offer metal, ceramic, zirconia, and porcelain-fused-to-metal crowns, each varying in durability, aesthetics, and cost.

Q: How long do dental fillings last?
A: Composite fillings typically last 5–7 years, while amalgam fillings can last 10–15 years with good oral care, though this varies by location in the mouth.

Q: Can a filling be done in a single visit?
A: Yes, fillings are almost always completed in a single visit unless the decay is extensive enough to require additional treatment first.

Q: Is tooth extraction painful?
A: Extractions are performed under local anesthesia, so the procedure itself is painless. Mild discomfort afterward is normal and manageable with prescribed pain relief.

Q: How long does recovery take after an extraction?
A: Most patients recover within 3–7 days, though wisdom tooth extractions may take slightly longer, especially if the tooth was impacted.

Q: Do you perform wisdom tooth extractions?
A: Yes, we handle both simple and surgical wisdom tooth extractions, including impacted teeth, with appropriate imaging beforehand to plan the procedure safely.

Q: What aftercare instructions should I follow after a procedure?
A: We provide written aftercare instructions specific to your treatment, covering diet restrictions, oral hygiene adjustments, and signs to watch for that may need follow-up.

Q: Who do I contact if I have concerns after a procedure?
A: You can call our clinic directly or message us on WhatsApp. For urgent post-treatment concerns outside hours, our emergency contact number is provided at discharge.

Q: Do you schedule follow-up appointments automatically?
A: For procedures like implants, root canals, or orthodontics, we schedule necessary follow-ups before you leave the clinic to ensure continuity of care.

Q: Is your clinic wheelchair accessible?
A: Yes, our clinic has a ramp at the entrance, an accessible washroom, and treatment rooms on the ground floor for patients with mobility needs.

Q: Is parking available at the clinic?
A: Yes, we have dedicated parking space for both two-wheelers and cars in front of the clinic, free of charge for patients.

A: WhatsApp is the fastest way to reach us for quick queries, while phone calls are best for appointment bookings and urgent matters.
